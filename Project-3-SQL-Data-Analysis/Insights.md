# Business Insights

## Insight 1: Luxury Brands Have the Highest Average Prices

Jaguar recorded the highest average vehicle price (~34,600), followed by Mercedes-Benz (~33,647) and Porsche (~31,400). This indicates that luxury brands command a significant premium compared to mainstream manufacturers.

## Insight 2: European Manufacturers Dominate the Premium Segment

Brands such as Jaguar, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche, BMW, and Volvo occupy the top positions in average vehicle pricing, highlighting the strong presence of European manufacturers in the luxury automobile market.

## Insight 3: Vehicle Distribution Is Uneven Across Brands

Dodge had the highest number of vehicles in the dataset, followed by BMW and Mercedes-Benz. This suggests that some manufacturers have greater representation than others within the dataset.

## Insight 4: Fuel Type Preferences Show Market Trends

The majority of vehicles in the dataset are gasoline-powered, indicating that gasoline remains the dominant fuel choice among listed automobiles.

## Insight 5: Body Style Distribution Reflects Consumer Demand

Certain body styles, such as sedans and hatchbacks, appear more frequently than others, suggesting higher consumer demand and market availability for these vehicle categories.

## Insight 6: Premium Vehicles Tend to Have Higher Performance

Brands with higher average prices generally also exhibit higher horsepower and larger engine sizes, indicating a relationship between performance specifications and vehicle pricing.

## Insight 7: Significant Price Variation Exists Across Manufacturers

The gap between luxury brands and economy brands demonstrates substantial variation in vehicle pricing, reflecting differences in positioning, features, and target customers.

## Insight 8: Above-Average-Priced Vehicles Represent a Premium Market Segment

Vehicles priced above the overall average represent a smaller but higher-value segment of the automobile market, dominated by luxury and performance-oriented manufacturers.
